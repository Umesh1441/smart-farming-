Government Policies for Farmers

Introduction

Welcome to our website that provides information about the various policies that the government brings for farmers. Our goal is to make it easy for farmers to learn about these policies and how they can benefit from them.

Recent Policies

1.PM VIshwakarma KAushal Samman (PM VIKAS) Under this scheme, artisans are provided financial assistance. The PM VIshwakarma KAushal Samman scheme will enable artisans to improve their products’ scale, quality, and reach, integrating them with the MSMEs value chain. Union Budget 2023: What is PM Vishwakarma Kaushal Samman?

2.Atmanirbhar Horticulture Clean Plant Programme This scheme will be helping in the availability of quality and disease-free planting material for high-value horticultural crops.

3. Pharma Innovation Programme In the Budget 2023, the Financial Minister proposed to launch a new program to promote innovation and research in pharmaceuticals, which will be taken up through centers of excellence.

4. Aspirational Districts and Blocks Programme The government has launched the Aspirational Blocks Programme, which will provide essential government services across multiple areas such as nutrition, health, agriculture, skill development, education, financial inclusion, water resources and basic infrastructure.

5.Pradhan Mantri PVTG Development Mission The Pradhan Mantri PVTG Development Mission will be launched to improve the socio-economic conditions of the Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs).

6. Green Credit Programme The Green Credit Programme will be launched under the Environment (Protection) Act to encourage behavioral change and will incentivize environmentally responsible and sustainable actions by individuals, companies and local bodies. It will assist them in mobilizing additional resources for such activities.

7. PM-PRANAM PM Programme for Restoration, Awareness, Nourishment and Amelioration of Mother Earth (PM-PRANAM) will incentivize states and UTs to promote the balanced use of chemical fertilizers and alternative fertilizers.

8. Amrit Dharohar Under this scheme the wetlands would be conserved by promoting their optimal use · The scheme is to be implemented in the next three years as Wetlands are crucial ecosystems to sustain biological diversity.
